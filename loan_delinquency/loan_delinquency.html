üè¶ Business Context: Why Loan Delinquency Matters

In banking and lending institutions, understanding borrower behavior is critical to managing credit risk. One key indicator of borrower risk is loan delinquency ‚Äî when a borrower misses scheduled payments.

A delinquent loan refers to a loan where the borrower has missed one or more payments, but the loan hasn‚Äôt yet defaulted.

Delinquency is an early warning signal of potential default.

Banks and lenders use delinquency metrics to:

  • Adjust credit policies
  • Refine credit scoring models
  • Launch early intervention strategies (e.g., calls, repayment plans)
  • Manage capital provisioning (reserving money for potential losses)
  • Optimize collections and recovery processes

üß† What is Delinquency?

A loan becomes delinquent when the borrower fails to pay on the scheduled date.

Delinquency is typically measured in buckets:

  • 0‚Äì30 days: Mild delinquency
  • 31‚Äì60 days: Moderate risk
  • 61‚Äì90+ days: Severe risk or default likely

In this simulation, we define delinquency as any loan with at least one missed payment over the observed period.

üìâ What Causes Delinquency?

  • Low credit score or poor credit history
  • Over-borrowing relative to income
  • High interest rates increasing EMI burden
  • Job loss, emergencies, or economic downturns
  • Lack of financial discipline

üîπ Simulating Borrower and Payment Data

We generate borrower-level monthly data for 24 months (2022‚Äì2023), simulating salary, EMI obligations, and monthly payments. This serves as our base behavioral dataset.

üîπ Features in Our Dataset

The dataset includes the following key variables:
  • customer_id: Unique identifier for the borrower
  • month: Year-month of observation
  • monthly_salary: Simulated monthly salary
  • monthly_emi: Simulated EMI burden
  • paid_emi: Actual amount paid by the customer
  • dpd: Days past due (calculated if full EMI is not paid)
  • bucket: Categorization of delinquency: Current, 30+, 60+, 90+, Write-off

üîπ Business Logic for Simulation

  • Borrowers are assigned EMI based on a percentage of their salary, typically 30‚Äì40%.
  • We randomly introduce missed or partial payments for certain months based on defined risk probabilities.
  • If EMI paid is less than due, we calculate a DPD and move customers to delinquency buckets.
  • Once a borrower enters delinquency, future months carry forward the overdue unless repaid.
