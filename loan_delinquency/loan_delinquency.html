Step 1: Introduction and Business Context

🎯 Objective of the Project

This project simulates a realistic loan portfolio and borrower behavior to study loan delinquency. Using synthetic data generated with the Faker library and logical rules, we replicate how different borrower profiles influence missed payments. The simulation lays the foundation for risk modeling and credit behavior analysis in banking.

🏦 Business Context: Why Loan Delinquency Matters

In lending institutions, understanding borrower repayment behavior is crucial to managing credit risk. One early signal of potential loan default is delinquency, where a borrower misses one or more payments. Banks closely monitor delinquency to:

  • Adjust credit policies
  • Launch early intervention and recovery efforts
  • Update credit scoring and decision models
  • Estimate capital requirements for loss provisioning

🧠 What is Delinquency?

A loan is considered delinquent when the borrower fails to pay on the due date. It is typically categorized into risk buckets:

  • 0–30 days: Mild delinquency
  • 31–60 days: Moderate risk
  • 61–90+ days: Severe risk / likely default

In this simulation, any loan with at least one missed payment is considered delinquent.

📉 What Causes Delinquency?

  • Low credit scores or poor credit history
  • High debt burden compared to income
  • Unforeseen events (job loss, emergencies)
  • Rising interest rates increasing EMIs
  • Lack of repayment discipline

🧩 How This Project Helps

This simulation enables:

  • Development of credit risk models like Probability of Default (PD)
  • Customer segmentation and risk targeting
  • What-if simulations for credit policy changes
  • Portfolio monitoring dashboards and alerts
