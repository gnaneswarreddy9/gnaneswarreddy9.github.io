ğŸ” What Happens After Default?

ğŸ“Œ Key Outcomes After Default

Once a borrower enters default, they are tagged with one of the following paths:

  • Charged-Off: The loan is written off as a loss after a certain period of non-recovery (e.g., 120+ days past due).
  • In Collections: Borrower remains delinquent and the loan is transferred to collections for recovery.
  • Re-defaulted: A borrower who was marked "Recovered" but defaults again later.

These outcomes help simulate a complete credit lifecycle beyond just predicting default.

ğŸ› ï¸ Modeling the Recovery Process

  • Charge-Off: If the borrower stays at 60 DPD or higher for more than 4 months.
  • Collections: If there's no payment activity for 2+ months post-default but charge-off criteria isn't met.
  • Re-default: If a borrower recovers but later returns to 30 or 60 DPD status.

This tagging logic is based on practical thresholds used by financial institutions.
